Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common safari planning questions

The best time varies by destination and what you want to see. Generally, dry seasons (June-October and December-March) offer excellent game viewing. For the Great Migration, timing depends on the specific crossing points you want to witness. We'll help you plan the perfect timing for your interests.

Our packages typically include accommodation, all meals, park fees, professional guide, transport in 4WD vehicles, and airport transfers. Gorilla permits, international flights, visas, and personal items are usually separate. We'll provide a detailed breakdown for your specific safari.

Safety is our top priority. We use experienced guides, well-maintained vehicles, and established accommodations. All activities follow strict safety protocols. We provide comprehensive pre-safari briefings and maintain constant communication throughout your journey.

Yellow fever vaccination is required for entry to Uganda and Rwanda. We recommend consulting your doctor or travel clinic 6-8 weeks before travel for personalized advice on malaria prevention and other recommended vaccinations based on your itinerary.
