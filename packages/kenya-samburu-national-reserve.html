Meet the Special Five

Unique wildlife species found only in Kenya's northern regions

Grevy's Zebra

The largest zebra species with distinctive narrow stripes and rounded ears. Critically endangered with only about 2,000 remaining worldwide.

Reticulated Giraffe

Distinguished by its net-like pattern of cream lines separating dark patches. Also known as the Somali giraffe, endemic to northern Kenya.

Beisa Oryx

A straight-horned antelope perfectly adapted to arid conditions. Known for its striking black and white facial markings and impressive horns.

Gerenuk & Somali Ostrich

The "giraffe gazelle" stands on hind legs to reach high branches. Plus the blue-necked Somali ostrich, smaller than its southern cousin.

Full Day Safari

Special Five Safari & River Activities

Begin with an early morning game drive when animals are most active. This is prime time to spot the elusive leopards and search for the Special Five. The morning light provides excellent photography opportunities. Return to the lodge for breakfast and a brief rest during the hot midday hours. After lunch, enjoy activities along the Ewaso Nyiro River - watch elephants bathing, hippos lounging, and crocodiles basking. Late afternoon game drive focuses on different areas of the reserve to maximize wildlife sightings. Sundowner drinks in the bush before returning to the lodge.

Photography Tip: Early morning and late afternoon provide the best lighting for wildlife photography in the dramatic Samburu landscapes.

Cultural Experience

Samburu Cultural Experience & Wildlife Tracking

Morning game drive followed by a visit to a traditional Samburu village (manyatta). Meet the Samburu people, known for their colorful dress, intricate beadwork, and warrior traditions. Learn about their semi-nomadic lifestyle, traditional dances, and how they coexist with wildlife. Participate in traditional activities like fire-making and understand their deep connection to the land and animals. Return to the lodge for lunch. Afternoon dedicated to tracking specific animals based on your interests - whether it's following elephant herds, searching for big cats, or focusing on the Special Five species. Evening at leisure with optional night game drive to spot nocturnal species.

Cultural Respect: Visits to Samburu communities are conducted with full respect for their traditions and with direct benefit to the community.

Final Game Drive & Return to Nairobi

Early morning game drive for any final wildlife sightings and photography opportunities. This is often the best time to spot animals you may have missed, including the elusive caracal, African wild dog (if you're lucky), or additional Special Five species. Return to the lodge for a hearty breakfast and check out. Begin the journey back to Nairobi with packed lunch and scenic stops along the way. Arrive in Nairobi by late afternoon with incredible memories of Kenya's northern wilderness, unique wildlife encounters, and authentic cultural experiences that few visitors get to experience.
